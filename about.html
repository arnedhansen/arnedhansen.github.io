About me

I am a PhD student in Cognitive Neuroscience jointly enrolled in the doctoral program in Psychology at the University of Zurich and the International PhD Program in Neuroscience at the Center for Neuroscience Zurich (ZNZ). My dissertation, «EEG Signatures of Oculomotor Control», investigates how neural oscillations — in particular alpha rhythms — relate to eye movements and attentional processes during working memory maintenance.

My research centres on the framework of OculoCortical Coherence (OCC): the idea that brain rhythms relate to cognition through oculomotor action. I develop automated signal processing pipelines for large-scale EEG and eye-tracking datasets, build statistical and computational models, and champion open-science workflows to support transparent and reproducible analysis.

I hold an MSc in Psychology (summa cum laude, 5.6) and a BSc in Psychology from the University of Zurich, both with a focus on neuropsychology and cognitive neuroscience. During my studies I gained extensive hands-on experience in EEG, psychophysiology, tACS, and eye-tracking experiments across multiple research groups.

Beyond research, I serve as Managing Director of the Competence Center for Language & Medicine Zurich, am an elected student representative on the Institute's Assembly and IT & Data Management Commission, and contribute to the psychology student magazine aware as an author and lector.

Research Interests

  • Oculomotor control & eye-tracking methodology
  • Working memory & attentional mechanisms
  • OculoCortical Coherence (OCC) framework
  • Open science, reproducibility & multiverse analysis
